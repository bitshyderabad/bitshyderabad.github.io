What to Expect From TEDx

The Daily Bitsian, Issue 14

Sunday, September 21, 2014. Not a soul stirred on that cold morning, especially at BITS Pilani, Hyderabad. This was the one day that people wouldn’t berate the students for being ‘self-taught’. Yet, there was an incessant buzz around the auditorium building, what with people moving about in black tees carrying a strangely satisfied look on their face . Ah yes, TEDx BITS Hyderabad was here.

The Climax

The 'Share an Idea with TED' part of the event included three young thinkers who presented their works and deserve a special mention indeed. Inching to his early twenties, Kaustubh Shivdikar developed an app 'Magic Pillow' to materialize those bedtime stroies which had a sculpting effect on his childhood, and combining his technical knowledge, he has been successfull in virtually viewing his bedtime stories in real perspective. This innovation is exemplary of truly going back to one's roots. On another note, Shiveesh Fotedar tackles his fears by harbouring a positive attitude and turning it in to fun. He works for the development of the app 'Roshni' which is basically a naviagation app for the visually impaired. Further, Rashida Taskink talks about her research on cerebral palsy and her first step to assist the victims. She calls it Spoonful and it effectively is a device for feeding patients with cerebral palsy.
